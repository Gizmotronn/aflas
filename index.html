Supporting mental health

What's the problem?

Mental illness is very common. One in 5 (20%) Australians aged 16-85 experience a mental illness in any year. The most common mental illnesses are depressive, anxiety and substance use disorder. Suicide in Australia Every day, at least 6 Australians die from suicide and a further thirty people will attempt to take their own life. Suicide is the leading cause of death for people Australians aged 25-44 and second leading cause of death for young people aged 15-24. Australians are more likely to die by suicide than skin cancer. Men are at greatest risk of suicide but least likely to seek help. In 2011 men accounted for over three quarters (76%) of deaths from suicide. However, an estimated 72% of males don’t seek help for mental disorders. It is recognised that the number of suicides and attempted suicides is likely to be underreported for several reasons including the practical problems of determining a person’s intentions, reporting problems and the stigma around suicide and self-harm.

Why Lifeline?

Lifeline WA’s mission is to prevent suicide, support people in crisis and reduce the stigmas, which can be a barrier to people seeking help. Lifeline WA provides all Western Australians experiencing a personal crisis or thinking about suicide with access to 24 hour crisis support and suicide prevention services. Their 13 11 14 service is a nationally-routed crisis support number, available every day of the year, from any location in Australia. It is available to anyone - regardless of age, gender, ethnicity, religion or sexual orientation.

Why HappinessCo?

The Happiness Co Foundation is formed to help the community "find their happy". Events, activations, programs and support groups for those affected by unhappiness and the Challenges on Mental Health.
Happiness CO Foundation’s goal is to unite people and to create opportunities for a better world. The Happiness Co Foundation is focused on making a positive difference in a challenging world.
